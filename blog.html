Minsk Belarus Vlog

Minsk Belarus Traveling Vlog - Iman GHR My second traveling Vlog hope you guys enjoy it.

My friend Anna told me about Minsk and I got very curious to visit one day. So 2 weeks ago I saw an empty spot on my schedule and I was like well why not give my friend a visit and see Minsk? And that is how it started, she helped me to find a good place to stay. She is from Minsk so she knows what area is close to the center and if the price is ok.

First day the weather was amazing and I was walking around with a short sleeve t-shirt witch i think it was kinda unusual for others since everyone else was in jackets. The temperature was 16 degrees at that day and it was the same as Helsingborg were I live but it felt warmer and sunlight was stronger if that makes any sense :D

Next day become a bit windy and a bit raid and third day was raining like hell so i guess i experienced almost all of it in only 4 days.

Most cafe places have WiFi and you can ask them to connect you if you are having trouble. The airport also has Wifi but you have to have a phone number that you are able to get an sms code to activate your WiFi if you are not able to get texts for any reason then no internet.

If you are going to take a taxi use Uber so you know how much it's going to cost but if you have to take a taxi make sure you ask them first how much it going to cost then accept the ride. Once they charged me almost 4 times higher than usual because I didn't ask. On the way to the airport I paid 36 BLR but on the way back he charged me for 130 BLR. His explanation was that the taxi here is like hotels, some places you pay more some place you pay less witch it was funny because the car was less comfy and was older.

Visa Issues:

I was traveling with Swedish pass and for traveling to Belarus we don't need a visa for Belarus neither a transit visa if we are flying to Mosco first then to Belarus but apparently if you flying back you need to have a transit visa from Belarus to Moscaw. So flying Copenhang to Moscaw then to Minsk no problem but flying from Minsk to Moscaw then to Copenhag you need to get a visa. Apparently there are some dramas between the 2 countries.

Foods are great, I had one of the greatest pizza I ever had in Minsk.

It was safe and I never had any problem with anything, not everyone speaks english but you find at least one person in any store or area that can speak english.

Night lives are cool and fun people are friendly a lot of bars and restaurants to visit.

If I missed anything or you have any questions ask me in the comment section I read all my comments.

Thanks for watching my Minsk Vlog!
